Card Metrics: How should compliance cards display data?

Currently CIS cards show pass rate (%) and non-CIS cards show coverage (%). We need a unified approach.

A

Unified Pass Rate

All cards show pass rate as the big number. Coverage becomes the subtitle.

87.3%
CIS E3 L1
123 of 140 assessed
83.3%
NIST Moderate
10 of 12 mapped passed
66.7%
SOC 2 TSC
8 of 12 mapped passed

Pros

  • Consistent metric across all cards
  • Pass rate is actionable ("how compliant am I?")
  • Matches CIS behavior users already understand

Cons

  • Hides coverage gap (unmapped controls are invisible)
  • High pass rate with low coverage can be misleading
B

Dual Metric (Pass Rate + Coverage)

All cards show pass rate as the big number, with a small coverage bar underneath showing how much of the framework catalog is mapped.

87.3%
CIS E3 L1
123 of 140 assessed
88% coverage
83.3%
NIST Moderate
10 of 12 mapped passed
4% coverage (12/325)
66.7%
SOC 2 TSC
8 of 12 mapped passed
15% coverage (12/80)

Pros

  • Shows both dimensions: "how well" and "how much"
  • Coverage bar gives honest picture without dominating
  • Pass rate is the primary metric (consistent)

Cons

  • More visual complexity per card
  • Coverage bar may be tiny for frameworks we don't cover well yet
C

Coverage Primary (Current Non-CIS Approach, Applied to All)

All cards show coverage as the big number. Pass/fail details appear on hover or in the matrix below.

88%
CIS E3 L1
123 of 140 mapped
4%
NIST Moderate
12 of 325 mapped
15%
SOC 2 TSC
12 of 80 mapped

Pros

  • Honest about our actual coverage
  • Motivates expanding framework mappings

Cons

  • Low percentages look alarming to clients
  • Doesn't show pass/fail of what IS assessed
  • CIS going from "87% pass rate" to "88% coverage" loses compliance info
